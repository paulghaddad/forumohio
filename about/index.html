About Us

Dr. Bob Stinson

stinson@forumohio.com

Bob Stinson, Psy.D., J.D., LICDC-CS, ABPP, is a board certified forensic psychologist, an attorney at law, and a licensed independent chemical dependency counselor – clinical supervisor. Dr. Stinson is listed in the National Register of Health Service Providers in Psychology. He is a Fellow of the American Academy of Forensic Psychology (AAFP) and a Diplomate in forensic psychology with the American Board of Professional Psychology (ABPP). Dr. Stinson worked for 15 years on a forensic unit at Twin Valley Behavioral Healthcare (an inpatient psychiatric hospital, serving the severely mentally ill), where he also served on the hospital’s Ethics Committee for a number of years, chairing it for his last two years. Now, Dr. Stinson is the Chief of Behavioral Health Services for the Ohio Department of Youth Services. He provides training and supervision to students, interns, and post-doctoral residents. He lectures across the country in the areas of mental health law and ethics. He is an Adjunct Professor of Clinical Psychology at The Ohio State University Department of Psychology and Wright State University’s School of Professional Psychology, and he is an Adjunct Professor of Law at The Ohio State University’s Moritz College of Law. Dr. Stinson holds membership in several legal and psychological professional associations, including APA (and several of its divisions), OPA (where he serves on the Board as President-Elect and is a member and past-chair of the Ethics Committee), and COPA (where he once served as the President of the organization). Dr. Stinson also maintains a private practice, providing consultations and evaluations, specializing in clinical and forensic psychology.

Dr. John L. Tilley

tilley@forumohio.com

John Tilley, Psy.D., earned his Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) in psychology from Capital University and his Doctor of Psychology (Psy.D.) in clinical psychology from the University of Indianapolis. As part of his graduate training, Dr. Tilley completed formal practica in forensic psychology at Twin Valley Behavioral Healthcare in Dayton and child/adolescent psychology at St. Francis Hospital in Indianapolis. His internship in correctional psychology was completed at the Federal Medical Center in Lexington. He completed his post-doctoral training in forensic psychology at Twin Valley Behavioral Healthcare in Columbus. Dr. Tilley is the chief executive officer of Behavioral Science Specialists, LLC and co-owner of Forum Ohio, LLC, which provide behavioral health, medical, and forensic assessment, consultative, educational, and research services. He regularly conducts psychological evaluations for a variety of agencies and court systems in the Columbus metropolitan area, including the Franklin County Department of Job and Family Services, the Franklin County Municipal Court, Twin Valley Behavioral Healthcare, the Franklin County Probate Court, the Franklin County Court of Common Pleas Probation Department, and the Franklin County Office on Aging. He has been an adjunct clinical faculty member at Capital University since 2001 where he teaches courses on psychology, including introductory psychology and forensic psychology. He is also a member of the Ohio Psychological Association, the Central Ohio Psychological Association, and the American Psychology-Law Society.

Dr. Kathryn Bell

bell@forumohio.com

Dr. Kathryn Bell completed her Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) in psychology at Capital University and went on to receive her Doctorate of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in clinical psychology at Western Michigan University. During her graduate work, she received extensive training, experience, and supervision in diagnosis, assessment, and evidence-based treatment for childhood and adult disorders, and completed a formal practicum through the Partial Hospitalization Program at Borgess Medical Center in Kalamazoo, Michigan. She completed her clinical internship through the Boston Consortium, including an 8-month rotation through Behavioral Sciences Division of the National Center for PTSD and a 4-month rotation through the Brockton VA Medical Center’s Alcohol and Drug Treatment Program and Project CALM (a treatment program for couples with co-occurring addictions and relationship problems). Following her internship, Dr. Bell completed a two-year clinical research postdoctoral fellowship within The Warren Alpert Medical School of Brown University working as Project Director for a clinical trial study of an HIV-risk reduction intervention for pregnant, drug-abusing women. She then went on to her first tenure-track assistant professor position within the Clinical Psychology doctoral program at Northern Illinois University. Dr. Bell is currently Associate Professor in Psychology at Capital University in Columbus, Ohio. As a Capital professor, Dr. Bell teaches courses in abnormal psychology, counseling, and the history of psychology, coordinates the psychology internships, and mentors undergraduate scholarship. She has widely presented and published on various research topics including intimate partner violence, sexual assault, trauma, and addictions. Dr. Bell regularly conducts psychological evaluations for the Social Security Administration and Veteran Evaluation Services. She serves as a regular reviewer for several specialty journals on violence and trauma and is a member of the Ohio Psychological Association, the Association for Behavioral and Cognitive Therapies, and the International Society for Traumatic Stress Studies.

Ms. Allison Chapman

chapman@forumohio.com

Allison Chapman, M.A. has a bachelor's degree in neuroscience from Kenyon College and a master's degree in cognitive psychology from The Ohio State University. Her awards and scholarships include Kenyon College Academic Scholar and The Ohio State University's University Fellowship. She has completed a number of EEG and empirical research projects investigating the computational structure of memory, and is a published author in the Journal of Memory and Language. Since completing her graduate studies, she has worked as an adjunct professor of psychology at Columbus State Community College. At Forum Ohio, Ms. Chapman is a psychology assistant, providing services under the supervision of the licensed psychologists.

Dr. Jeremy D. Kaufman

kaufman@forumohio.com

Jeremy D. Kaufman, Psy.D. earned his B.S. from The Ohio State University and his M.A. and Psy.D. in Clinical Psychology from the Georgia School of Professional Psychology. Dr. Kaufman completed an internship at Oregon Health and Science University, a LEND (Leadership Education in Neurodevelopmental and Related Disabilities) program. He completed his post-doctoral training in Pediatric Psychology with a focus on children with mental retardation and developmental disabilities and their families at Nationwide Children’s Hospital/The Ohio State University. He has been the Director of Psychological Health for the Ohio National Guard. Currently, he provides psychological services at the Veteran's Administration, Behavioral Science Specialists, LLC, and Forum Ohio, LLC. Dr. Kaufman provides psychological evaluation and treatment services to adults and children, particularly from a behavioral perspective. He is a member of the American Psychological Association and the Ohio Psychological Association.
